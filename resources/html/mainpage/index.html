Human Uniqueness website

The explanation of human uniqueness is not to be found in a single triggering event or cause in the past, but instead is an outcome of a complex set of interacting events and processes. The implication is that human origins scientists must cross domains to jointly define objectives, design research questions, and evaluate results. Equally important, they must jointly articulate the hypotheses from theoretical propositions about human uniqueness and the test implications for the empirical evidence sought in the field and laboratory. We cannot forecast exactly when or in what shape a synthetic explanation of human uniqueness will emerge, but the transdisciplinary research tool we are attempting here is one that may produce new understanding. The central goal of this website is to test predictions from recently generated theoretical propositions about human uniqueness with the empirical evidence on the timing of appearance and patterns of association of key human adaptations recovered from field and laboratory research.

Institute of Human Origins

The Institute of Human Origins is one of the preeminent research organizations in the world devoted to the science of human origins. A research center of the Arizona State University College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, IHO pursues an integrative strategy for research and discovery central to its 30-year-old founding mission, bridging social, earth, and life science approaches to the most important questions concerning the course, causes, and timing of events in the human career over deep time. IHO fosters public awareness of human origins and its relevance to contemporary society through innovative outreach programs that create timely, accurate information for both education and lay communities.
